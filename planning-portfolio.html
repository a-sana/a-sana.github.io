I helped with facilitating the SwissCal Conference on the Swiss Public Transportation Ecosystem. I wrote three reports; one for SCAG, which you can view here; one for LA Metro (not available to the public); and one for the general Bay Area region (also not available to the public). There were six sessions total, held on different days; the first three were the main sessions, and the final three were region-specific workshops. In addition to the reports, I authored three blog posts for the main sessions, which you can view here, here, and here; I also regularly updated and maintained the conference website. Additionally, the blog posts are referenced here in an article by Kylie Gordon of the Bill Lane Center for the American West at Stanford University.

I used a GPS to note all locations where I encountered different art pieces. I then categorized the types of art that I found. I created this map using ArcGIS and Adobe Illustrator for my Cartography class at UC Berkeley.

For my Urban Planning Studio course, I analyzed census data to determine the highest concentration of Latine individuals in San Mateo County. I created the maps by hand using Adobe Illustrator and created the layout in Adobe InDesign.

For my Urban Planning Studio course, we worked with a group of mothers advocating to improve their community in San Jose. I helped them outline immediate and short-term goals.

For my Geographic Information Systems (GIS) course, my group and I conducted a suitability analysis to determine the best location for a new skatepark in San Francisco. I helped gather and organize and gather the data and conduct the data analysis to create the maps.
